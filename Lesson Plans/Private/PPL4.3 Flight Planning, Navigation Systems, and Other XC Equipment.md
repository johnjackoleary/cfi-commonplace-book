### Introduction
The crux of cross-country planning is choosing a route, which includes waypoints, altitudes, and estimates of time and fuel consumption.  In this lesson we'll talk about all the considerations in choosing a route.  We'll also talk about radio navigation aids (such as VORs and GPS), some onboard radio equipment, understanding the autopilot, and the ATC services available en route.

1. Planning a cross-country route
	1. Charting a [[Course]]: [[Sectional Chart with Checkpoints.jpeg]], considering:
		1. Hazards and terrain along the course
		2. Airspaces to avoid
		3. Good engine-out options
		4. Passenger comfort
	2. Choosing checkpoints
		1. The best checkpoints can be verified visually, and seem from a distance if you're off course
	3. Choosing altitude
		1. High enough to avoid obstacles
		2. Higher altitudes can have smoother air, or better fuel efficiency
		3. Headwinds and tailwinds generally increase speed at higher altitude
	4. Day-of considerations
		1. Winds aloft may favor different course/altitude
		2. Clouds, ice, turbulence, etc may influence course/altitude

### Completion Standards
Client should develop knowledge of navigation equipment on board their training aircraft. Client should be familiar with services available from ATC, and know how and when to request assistance.
