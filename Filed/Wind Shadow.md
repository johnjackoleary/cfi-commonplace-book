***Definition***    :: When an object blocks the wind, creating an area of no wind.
***Source***         :: [BOAT CREW HANDBOOK – Seamanship Fundamentals](https://www.uscgaux-ocnj.org/Training/Crew%20Manuals/BOAT%20CREW%20HANDBOOK%2***0-%2016114.4A_Seamanship%20Fundamentals.pdf)

> [!quote] From [[Seaplane, Skiplane, and Float or Ski Equipped Helicopter Operations Handbook|Seaplane Handbook]] Chap 6
> There is usually a glassy band of calm water on the upwind shore of a lake.

> [!quote] From [Go To Hull](https://seaplanepilotsassociation.org/wp-content/uploads/GoToHull.pdf)
> If the wind is light or strong, it will show a wind shadow next to the offshore wind. The wind can't drop right down over the trees or weeds or a bank and ripple the water. The wind shadow will be glassy water.
