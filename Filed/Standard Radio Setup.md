There are many correct radio setups you might use.

## Pre-Solo
Jack recommends this for pre-solo students or pattern work

| Radio  | Active | Standby |
| ------ | ------ | ------- |
| COM 1 | TWR    | 121.5 (or practice area frequency)   |
| COM 2 | GND    | ATIS    |

This ensures you're tested on both COM 1 and COM 2 before takeoff, and starts to build good radio hygiene for cross-country flights.

## Cross-Country
As you start into cross-countries, you may consider COM 2 for listening and COM 1 for talking & listening.^[Said differently, using COM 2 for only ATIS, 121.5, or similar monitoring].

> [!tip] 
> Jack still always makes at least one [[tx]] & [[rx]] on COM 2 to test it's working.

## Comparison to Airliners
As your procedural complexity increases, your radio setups may change.

> [!example] 
> Here is a radio setup you might see on an airliner.
> 
> COM 1
> - TWR
> - GND
> - En Route (dep/CTR/Apr)
> 
> COM 2
> - ATIS/AWOS
> - Ramp
> - Clearance Delivery
> - Guard up in flight
